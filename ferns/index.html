What is a fern?

A fern is a type of vascularized plant. This means that it has specialized tissues that transports water, nutrients and sugars throughout the fern's body [1]. Specifically, a fern uses the xylem system. This system uses pressurized system to transport sap through a channel formed of dead plant cells [5].

Ferns are different from most plants that we are used to seeing because they reproduce through spores rather than seeds. Additionally, most ferns are rhizomes [4]. So many ferns are actually connected through underground stems, which reach out to form the plants that we see.
